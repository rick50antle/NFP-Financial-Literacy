Who This Is For

Board members, trustees, and finance committee members who need to understand nonprofit financial statements. Basic familiarity with for-profit financials is helpful but not required.

📊

Real Data, Real Insight

Every question uses actual financial data from the Oregon Humane Society and LEAP. You'll work with the same numbers a real board member would see.

🧠

Understanding First

This series focuses on understanding what the statements mean, not ratio analysis. You'll learn to read before you calculate. A future series covers analysis.

Core Ideas

What You'll Master

⚖

The Accounting Identity

Assets − Liabilities = Net Assets. The identity that structures every nonprofit statement.

🔒

Restrictions vs. Designations

Donor-imposed restrictions and board designations look similar but work very differently.

🔗

Cross-Statement Connections

Four financial statements telling one integrated story through the accounting identity.

💧

Liquidity

Not "do we have sufficient reserves?" but "can we pay our bills?" The question boards need to ask.

🔍

Understanding First

Read the statements before running ratios. Understanding makes analysis meaningful.
